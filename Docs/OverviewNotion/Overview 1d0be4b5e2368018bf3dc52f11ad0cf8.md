# Overview

- **App Description**:
    - An AI-powered deck analysis platform for **Magic: The Gathering** players. Built with scalability, player engagement, and cutting-edge ML technology in mind. This project follows a **phased development roadmap**, starting with a robust MVP and scaling into advanced AI-powered insights.
- **Feature List**:
    
    ### **Phase 1: Core Functionality (MVP - Minimum Viable Product)**
    
    **Objective:** Deliver a functional version of the app that can analyze Magic: The Gathering decks based on basic input and produce useful insights.
    
    1. **User Registration & Authentication**
        - Allow users to sign up, log in, and manage accounts.
        - Include basic profile management (username, email, etc.).
    2. **Deck Input**
        - Implement a simple deck submission form (input via text, file upload, or link).
        - Support for common deck formats like **decklist text** and **MTG XML format**.
        - Ability to save and manage submitted decks in user profiles.
    3. **Deck Validation**
        - Validate the submitted deck for errors (e.g., duplicate cards, illegal cards, missing cards, etc.).
        - Provide feedback on deck legality for various formats (e.g., Standard, Modern, Commander).
    4. **Basic Deck Analysis (Stats Overview)**
        - Show the basic statistics for the deck (e.g., card distribution, mana curve, average cost, and colors).
        - Provide a simple analysis of card types (creature, sorcery, artifact, etc.) in the deck.
    5. **Deck Export**
        - Allow users to export the analyzed deck in a common format (e.g., plain text, CSV, or MTG XML).
    6. **Basic UI Design**
        - Simple and intuitive UI with basic sections for deck input, analysis results, and deck management.

- **Target Audience**: Identify your users (e.g., competitive Magic: The Gathering players, hobbyists, etc.).
    
    ### **Competitive Magic: The Gathering Players**
    
    - **Description**: These users participate in local and major tournaments, both online and offline. They are highly invested in improving their decks and performance in competitions.
    - **Needs**:
        - Advanced deck analysis tools to optimize their decks for specific formats (e.g., Modern, Standard, Commander).
        - AI-powered recommendations to improve card synergies, mana curves, and deck strengths.
        - Tools to compare their decks to top-performing tournament decks and track metagame trends.
        - Tournament simulation features to predict performance and practice matchups against common archetypes.
    - **Features of Interest**:
        - AI-driven deck analysis.
        - Meta forecasting and performance prediction.
        - Tournament deck archive.
        - Deck export and comparison tools.
    
    ### **2. Casual Players & Hobbyists**
    
    - **Description**: Players who enjoy Magic: The Gathering as a casual pastime. They might not focus on the competitive aspect as much but still care about creating fun, unique decks for social play or casual events.
    - **Needs**:
        - Easy-to-use deck analysis with a focus on fun and creative aspects.
        - Insights into how to build more balanced and thematic decks.
        - Simple, beginner-friendly recommendations for card suggestions and deck improvements.
    - **Features of Interest**:
        - Basic deck analysis (card distribution, mana curve, etc.).
        - Easy deck input methods.
        - Card suggestion tool for deck improvement.
        - Simple UI for non-technical users.
    
    ### **3. Deck Builders & Deck Collection Enthusiasts**
    
    - **Description**: These users are focused on building and refining their Magic decks, often for collection purposes or to experiment with new strategies. They may or may not be active in tournaments.
    - **Needs**:
        - Tools to manage and track multiple decks.
        - Insights into the best cards to add to their collection based on deck-building goals (e.g., synergy, value, or theme).
        - The ability to import, export, and save decks for future reference.
    - **Features of Interest**:
        - Deck management tools.
        - AI recommendations for deck improvements.
        - Price tracking for card values.
        - Deck export and collection tracking features.
    
    ### **4. Magic: The Gathering Enthusiasts & Content Creators**
    
    - **Description**: These users are passionate about the game and may create content (e.g., streamers, YouTubers, bloggers) related to Magic: The Gathering. They are interested in showcasing decks, strategies, and discussing the metagame.
    - **Needs**:
        - Tools to create and share decks easily with their followers.
        - Insightful analysis and comparison features for discussing deck strategies and performance.
        - Access to tournament-winning decks and strategies for content creation.
    - **Features of Interest**:
        - Deck sharing and collaboration tools.
        - Community features such as deck ratings and comments.
        - Deck comparison and meta analysis.
        - Integration with streaming platforms or social media for sharing decklists.
    
    ### **5. New or Casual Players (Beginners)**
    
    - **Description**: New to Magic: The Gathering or players who have limited experience. They might not fully understand the complexity of the game yet but are learning and building decks for fun.
    - **Needs**:
        - Easy onboarding with clear, step-by-step guides for creating decks.
        - Basic recommendations and explanations of deck components (e.g., mana curve, card types).
        - Support for casual formats like **Commander** or **Brawl**.
    - **Features of Interest**:
        - Beginner-friendly UI and guides.
        - Simple deck analysis (mana curve, card types).
        - Basic recommendations and educational resources.
        - Casual format support and deck-building tips.
    
    ### **6. Collectors and Investors**
    
    - **Description**: Users who are primarily focused on collecting Magic cards for investment purposes. They may not play the game but are interested in tracking card prices and trends for future value.
    - **Needs**:
        - Tools for tracking the market value of cards and overall deck prices.
        - Insights into the best investment opportunities in Magic cards.
        - Historical price trends for cards.
    - **Features of Interest**:
        - Card pricing and value analysis.
        - Integration with external services (e.g., TCGPlayer, Scryfall) for price tracking.
        - Trends and analysis of valuable cards.
    
    ---
    
    ### **Key Takeaways for User Personas**
    
    - **Competitive Players** need advanced AI analysis, deck comparison tools, tournament simulation, and metagame forecasting.
    - **Casual Players & Hobbyists** require simple deck analysis tools, card suggestions, and thematic deck building.
    - **Deck Builders & Collectors** benefit from deck management tools, AI recommendations, and price tracking.
    - **Content Creators & Enthusiasts** will be looking for deck-sharing capabilities, community features, and comparative analysis tools.
    - **New Players** need easy-to-understand deck-building advice, simple analysis, and format support for casual games.
    - **Collectors & Investors** want price tracking, market insights, and value analysis tools.
    
    ---
    
    By focusing on these user groups and understanding their distinct needs, you can tailor your app’s features, design, and marketing to meet their expectations, ensuring that your **MTG Deck Analyzer** is valuable to a wide range of Magic: The Gathering players and enthusiasts.
